# Evidence

## Demand reality

[Who specifically has this problem? What are they doing today? When was the last concrete occurrence?]

## Existing alternatives

[What tools/workarounds exist? Why are they insufficient?]

## Current user behavior

[What do people actually do today, not what they say they would do?]

## Concrete pain examples

[Specific stories with specific people losing specific things: time, money, attention, status, emotional energy.]

## Frequency / intensity

[How often does this problem occur? How much does each occurrence cost?]

## Willingness signals

[Evidence that people would adopt, pay, or switch. Actions, not words.]

## Risks of false positive

[Why might this be a "cool idea" rather than real demand?]

## Assumptions to test

[What are you assuming that could be wrong? How will you test each assumption? Opportunity-solution mapping: for each assumption, what is the smallest experiment that would invalidate it?]

## Discovery refresh cadence

[When will you next check whether this evidence still holds? Continuous discovery is not one-time. Set a date or milestone for the next evidence refresh.]

## Observation notes

[What did you observe real users doing? What surprised you? "Nothing surprising" means you weren't looking.]

## Evidence status

[ ] passed — specific user, concrete pain, named alternative, narrow wedge identified
[ ] weak — missing one or more required signals
[ ] failed — no concrete evidence of demand
[ ] waived_by_user — user explicitly chose to skip validation (reason must be in decisions.md)
